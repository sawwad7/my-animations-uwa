Dark Sailor Rum

Dark Sailor Rum

A cheap dark rum produced in and for the West African market. The brand name itself evokes the maritime trade networks — the 'dark sailor' who carried goods (and people) across the Atlantic. Rum was the currency that purchased enslaved bodies: European traders exchanged barrels of it for human beings at coastal forts. Its caps appear frequently in Anatsui's sculptures, their deep color forming the shadowed areas of his compositions — the darkness that underwrites the gold.

Seaman's Aromatic Schnapps

Seaman's Aromatic Schnapps

Originally a Dutch-produced spirit, Seaman's Schnapps became central to West African ritual life — used in libations, ceremonies, and gift-giving. This was not accidental: European traders deliberately aimed this product at African spiritual practices, weaponizing cultural integration as a market strategy. The 'schnapps ritual' is now inseparable from the colonial trade that introduced it. The gun was disguised as a gift.

Nigerian Guinness

Nigerian Guinness

Guinness opened its Lagos brewery in 1962, two years after Nigerian independence — timing that was strategic, not coincidental. Today, Nigeria is Guinness's second-largest market worldwide. The colonial barrel never stopped pointing: it simply rebranded. The ubiquity of these caps in Anatsui's material supply is evidence that the extraction economy didn't end — it incorporated.
